Excited to have presented some of my latest research at CSME-CFDSC-CSR 2026 in Vancouver this week!

I want to first thank Journal Fluids MDPI for giving me this opportunity through the Travel Award. Established in 2018, this annual global award supports postdoctoral fellows and PhD students in attending and presenting their research at international conferences in the field of fluid mechanics. 
https://lnkd.in/eze67eQa

My presentation focused on applying an array of five circular nozzle Synthetic Jet Actuators (SJAs) to control flow over a circular cylinder at Re_D = 3,900, with the goal of reducing flow separation. Two key findings stood out: the cumulative effect of the SJAs at mid-span, and that even a modest blowing ratio of C_B = 0.84 was sufficient to delay separation at a reduced frequency of F⁺ = 1.

In alignment with Journal Fluids MDPI's commitment to open science, a recorded version of the presentation has been uploaded to my personal YouTube channel: https://lnkd.in/e4eVbQfW
