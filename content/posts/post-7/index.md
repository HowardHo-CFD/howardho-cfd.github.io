The **David Sanborn Scott Graduate Fellowship** is awarded to graduate students in MIE for impactful research in Hydrogen and Low-Carbon Energy Systems, and I am grateful to be selected for this prestigious honor! 🌱

Dr. Scott's paper, *"Fossil Sources: Running Out Is Not the Problem"*, has been incredibly influential in shaping my perspective. The way it highlights environmental and geopolitical factors, rather than just resource scarcity, as the true challenges in the energy transition has stayed with me ever since I first read it. I highly recommend giving it a read! 💡

This incredible opportunity will support my research on **improving wind turbine efficiency through innovative Synthetic Jet Actuator (SJA) technology**. By developing advanced methods to control airflow separation around airfoils, we're taking meaningful steps toward Canada's 2050 net-zero emissions goal. 🌍

A huge thank you to Dr. Scott and the University of Toronto for believing in my research and supporting the next generation of sustainable energy innovators. This fellowship isn't just funding — it's a genuine contribution to a more sustainable future. ✨
